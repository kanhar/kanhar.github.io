New parent musings

Labor Not an ideal outcome to end up requiring a C-Section - but know that if you do, Anaesthesiologist's today recommend getting a Combined Spinal Epidural (CSE) over a regular epidural. A study from 2012 - Combined spinal-epidural versus epidural analgesia in labour indicated no substantial basis for one being better than the other - despite a slightly faster onset for CSE's. We declined the CSE opting instead for the Regular Epidural Level L4-L5. This was a particularly difficult decision to make this decision quickly and under duress. Recommend an expecting couple look into tradeoffs prior.

 

Fever Standard American Clinical Practice Guidelines (CPG's) for infants presenting with a fever under 29 days of age requires getting a Lumbar Puncture (LP) to rule out a Serious Bacterial Infection(SBI). LPs often carry side effects and are best avoided. After 29 days, there are certain exclusion criteria before requiring an LP, although not all hospitals adhere to this, see this & this.

 

Feeding were caught off guard with the variety of dynamic lactation challenges we experienced during the first two months. So much so that we recommend all expectant parents schedule a certified lactation consultant ( IBCLC provider ) to visit as soon as possible after birth. Most insurances should cover these visits - although significant legwork may be required in filing the necessary claims. For the Seattle area we worked with and recommend highly Judy Jewett - who helped us through multiple at-home visits.

 

Tongue Tie An unfortunate event ~15% of parents have to deal with - but one best worth being prepared for. A 10x rise in tongue tie surgeries makes this feel like a fad - and the evidence is not fully supportive of intervention. See PMC 30001811 and PMC 33564576 Our primary pediatrician was reluctant to advocate for the frenectomy but secondary opinions from 2 naturopathic pediatrician concluded otherwise. With one suggesting a Craniosacral massage by a Registered Craniosacral Therapist (RCST) as a less invasive precursor, that works in 1 out of 4 cases. Hoping to minimize intervention we scheduled two of these massages, but the RCST provider ultimately recommended we get an official Frenectomy evaluation. We scheduled this evaluation and the assessment confirmed a tongue tie case.

A week later, we noticed the following improvements: daily feed durations went down from 200m to 130m. Daily feed session count went down from 9.5 to 8. Yet weight gain stayed linear and consistent. Given what we know now we are glad we did this surgery.
